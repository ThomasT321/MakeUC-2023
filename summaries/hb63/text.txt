To amend sections 3314.03, 3326.11, and 3328.24 and

Section 1. That sections 3314.03, 3326.11, and 3328.24 be

Sec. 3313.6030. (A) Beginning with the first full school
year that begins after the effective date of this section, each
city, exempted village, and local school district and chartered
nonpublic school shall provide classroom instruction on conflict
resolution to students in grades kindergarten through twelve.
The instruction shall be integrated into an appropriate course
of study as determined by each school.
(B) The conflict resolution instruction provided under
this section shall be age-appropriate and provide information,
including the following topics:
(1) Skills of conflict resolution, including methods of
diffusing conflict situations and resolving differences
amicably;
(2) The mediation process;
(3) Respect for and awareness of different cultures,
beliefs, religions, races, gender identities, and sexual
orientations, including empathy and compassion for others;
(4) Effective communication;
(5) Establishing and maintaining positive social
relationships;
(6) Effectively managing emotions;
(7) Making responsible decisions;
(8) The correlation between conflict and violence,
including gun violence.
(C) In developing and implementing the conflict resolution
instruction required under this section, districts and schools
may use available public-private partnerships, materials,
existing curricula or programs, or other resources.
(D) Not later than six months after the effective date of
this section, the department of education shall compile a list
of appropriate curricula and materials that districts and
schools may use to comply with the conflict resolution
instruction required under this section. The department shall
publish the list on its web site.
(E) Beginning with the first full school year that begins
after the effective date of this section, the department shall
make available to all districts and schools in-service training
programs based on the conflict resolution instruction required
under this section. The department shall periodically review and
modify the in-service training programs to provide the most
current information.
(F) Beginning with the first day of July of the first full
school year that begins after the effective date of this section
and every three years thereafter, each district and school shall
provide, as part of the school's in-service training, programs
based on the conflict resolution instruction requirements
established under this section for all teachers whose teaching
responsibilities include courses of study in which the mandated
conflict resolution instruction is integrated. A school may use
the in-service training programs developed by the department
under this section or alternative programs that are consistent
with the provisions of this section.
Training completed under this division shall qualify as a
professional development activity for the renewal of educator
licenses, in addition to activities approved by local
professional development committees under division (F) of
section 3319.22 of the Revised Code.
